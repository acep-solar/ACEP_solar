Welcome to ARCTIC

A tool developed in collaboration between the Alaska Center for Energy and Power and the UW Clean Energy Institute

The objective of this project is to calculate a number of different parameters and metrics for solar panels distributed throughout the state of Alaska, and generate an interactive graphic that enables users to investigate the costs and benefits to solar in their area, as well as provide a means to investigate if their panels are underperforming expected performance. This tool relies on a combination of a computational tool known as PVWatts that is produced by the National Renewable Energy Lab (NREL), and data generously shared with ACEP by individuals around the state.

PVWatts estimates the energy production and cost of energy for solar photovoltaic (PV) systems around the world, making easy for homeowners, small building owners, installers and manufacturers who want to estimate the performance of a potential PV installation.

TMY2 and TMY3 are two different data set from PVWatts containing climate information. They can estimate the solar production in Alaska.
