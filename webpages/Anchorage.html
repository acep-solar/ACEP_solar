Anchorage

Anchorage is in the south-central portion of Alaska, at the terminus of the Cook Inlet, on a peninsula formed by the Knik Arm to the north and the Turnagain Arm to the south. Anchorage is Alaska's most populous city and contains more than 40 percent of the state's total population. Anchorage has a subarctic climate but with strong maritime influences that lead to a relatively moderate climate. Most of its precipitation falls in late summer. PV Watts Prediction

The following 3 figures is the visualization of data from the PV Watts V6. We use the PV Watts API to call the prediction. All the following data are based on these hypothetic parameters:
(1) System Capacity: 4 kW
(2) Losses: 14%
(3) Dataset: TMY2 (Weather station name: Anchorage) ;TMY3 (Weather station name: Anchorage/Elmendorf)
(4) Inverter Efficiency: 96%
(5) lat:61.19 ; lon:-149.69
For anyone who wants to use this APP to see the different prediction by changing the parameters, you can go to this Website:https://pvwatts.nrel.gov/pvwatts.php.

1. Annual Production

Bokeh Plot

You can see the above figure that when you choose to set the tilt of you panel at about 45 degree in Anchorage, it has more chance to give you a max annual production at about 950 kWh. However, if you choose to set your panel vertically, it will still generate about 750 kWh power for a year. It is apparent that the prediction based on the TMY3 will give you higher value that TMY2.

2. Loss of Annual Production

Bokeh Plot

This figure is more clear to tell you how percentage of the annual production you will lose, compared with the tilt that will give you the max annual production.

3. The tendency of monthly production

Bokeh Plot

The figure showing the how much energy will be producted of each month. You can get the different tendency of energy be selecting different tilts below the figure. The x axis is month of a year and y axis is the energy production with unit kWh.
