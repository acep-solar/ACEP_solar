About Anchorage

Anchorage is in the south-central portion of Alaska, at the terminus of the Cook Inlet, on a peninsula formed by the Knik Arm to the north and the Turnagain Arm to the south. Anchorage is Alaska's most populous city and contains more than 40 percent of the state's total population. Anchorage has a subarctic climate but with strong maritime influences that lead to a relatively moderate climate. Most of its precipitation falls in late summer. Average daytime summer temperatures range from approximately 55 to 78 ℉ (13 to 26 ℃); average daytime winter temperatures are about 5 to 30 ℉ (-15.0 to -1.1 ℃).

--Data from Wikipedia

Rolling 12-Month Annual Production

Bokeh Plot

This graphic provides a rolling 12-month average. It shows the average of the previous 12-months of production, reported on a per-month basis. The data in this figure is normalized by the DC Capacity that is installed, so all panel production is comparable. This figure shows the fluctuation of the annual production. PVWatts data using both the TMY2 and TMY3 weather models are provided as reference to show what an optimal system with ideal performance and installation geometry should produce.

The Error Bar of Monthly Production

Bokeh Plot

This graphic shows the average standard deviation across all of the installations at this location. The data here is normalized by the installed DC Capacity. The blue line shows the standard deviation, and the red dot shows the mean of the production for that month. A low standard deviation indicates that the data points tend to be close to the mean (also called the expected value). PV Watts Prediction

The following 3 figures is the visualization of data from the PV Watts V6. We use the PV Watts API to call the prediction. All the following data are based on these hypothetic parameters:
(1) System Capacity: 4 kW
(2) Losses: 14%
(3) Dataset: TMY2 (Weather station name: Anchorage) ;TMY3 (Weather station name: Anchorage/Elmendorf)
(4) Inverter Efficiency: 96%
(5) lat:61.19 ; lon:-149.69
For anyone who wants to use this APP to see the different prediction by changing the parameters, you can go to this Website:https://pvwatts.nrel.gov/pvwatts.php.

1. Annual Production

Bokeh Plot

You can see the above figure that when you choose to set the tilt of you panel at about 45 degree in Anchorage, it has more chance to give you a max annual production at about 950 kWh. However, if you choose to set your panel vertically, it will still generate about 750 kWh power for a year. It is apparent that the prediction based on the TMY3 will give you higher value that TMY2.
