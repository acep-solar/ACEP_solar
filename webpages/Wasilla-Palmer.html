Wasilla-Palmer

Wasilla is a city in Matanuska-Susitna Borough, United States and the sixth-largest city in Alaska. It is located on the northern point of Cook Inlet in the Matanuska-Susitna Valley of the southcentral part of the state. The city's population was 7,831 at the 2010 census. Wasilla has a climate similar to that of Anchorage, but with slightly warmer daytime maxima and colder nighttime minima due to its inland location.

Palmer is a city in and the borough seat of the Matanuska-Susitna Borough in the U.S. state of Alaska. It is part of the Anchorage Metropolitan Statistical Area. As of the 2010 census, the population of the city is 5,937.Palmer has a climate similar to that of Anchorage, although with low temperatures that are on average 1.4 °„F (0.8 °„C) cooler and highs 0.8 °„F (0.4 °„C) warmer. August is the wettest month both by total precipitation and number of days with precipitation, and likewise, April is the driest.

Actual Data

1. Normalized Rolling Annual Production

Bokeh Plot

A rolling average is a calculation to analyze data points by creating a series of averages of different subsets of the full data set. In our figure, x axis is date, and y axis is AC kWh produced of last one year divided by DC kW installed. Every point presents the amount production of last 12 months. This figure shows the fluctuation of the annual production.

2. Normalized Monthly Production

Bokeh Plot

This is the monthly production figure. The x axis is date and y axis is AC kWh produced/DC kW installed (kWh/kW) of each month. We take the best performance data and median performance data to show. TMY2 and TMY3 lines are the prediction values from PVWatts which is a system they can collect solar source data and predict solar panel production. As you see, the production in the summer is pretty high because the sun exposes on the sky all the day, but the situation in the winter is opposite. Please click the right symbol to disappear or appear different lines.

3. The Error Bar of Monthly Production

Bokeh Plot

This is the error bar of Monthly production from all the solar PV arrays. The x axis is date and the y axis is ACkWh produced divided by DC kW installed. The red point is mean value of monthly production. The blue line is the standrad deviation of production distribution. A low standard deviation indicates that the data points tend to be close to the mean (also called the expected value) of the set, while a high standard deviation indicates that the data points are spread out over a wider range of values. Please click the right symbols to disappear or appear different lines.

PV Watts Prediction

The following 3 figures is the visualization of data from the PV Watts V6. We use the PV Watts API to call the prediction. All the following data are based on these hypothetic parameters:
(1) System Capacity: 4 kW
(2) Losses: 14%
(3) Dataset: TMY2 (Weather station name: Anchorage) ;TMY3 (Weather station name: Anchorage/Elmendorf)
(4) Inverter Efficiency: 96%
(5) lat:61.19 ; lon:-149.69
For anyone who wants to use this APP to see the different prediction by changing the parameters, you can go to this Website:https://pvwatts.nrel.gov/pvwatts.php.
