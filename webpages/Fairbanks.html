Welcome to ARCTIC

A tool developed in collaboration between the Alaska Center for Energy and Power and the UW Clean Energy Institute

Bokeh Plot

This is the monthly production figure. The x axis is month and y axis is the production of each month. We take the best performance data and median performance data to show. TMY2 and TMY3 lines are the prediction values from PVWatts which is a system they can collect solar source data and predict solar panel production. As you see, the production in the summer is pretty high because the sun exposes on the sky all the day, but the situation in the winter is opposite.

PV Watts Prediction

The following 3 figures is the visualization of data from the PV Watts V6. We use the PV Watts API to call the prediction. All the following data are based on these hypothetic parameters:
(1) System Capacity: 4 kW
(2) Losses: 14%
(3) Dataset: TMY2 (Weather station name: Fairbanks)
(4) Inverter Efficiency: 96%
For anyone who wants to use this APP to see the different prediction by changing the parameters, you can go to this Website:https://pvwatts.nrel.gov/pvwatts.php.
